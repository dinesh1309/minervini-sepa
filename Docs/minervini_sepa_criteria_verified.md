## Part 1: Trend Template — Stage 2 Uptrend Confirmation

These 8 criteria must ALL be met for a stock to qualify:

| # | Criterion | Exact Specification |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Price vs 150-DMA & 200-DMA | Stock price is **above both** the 150-day and 200-day moving averages |
| 2 | 150-DMA vs 200-DMA | The 150-day MA is **above** the 200-day MA |
| 3 | 200-DMA Trend | 200-day MA line is **trending up** for at least 1 month (preferably 4-5 months minimum) |
| 4 | 50-DMA Position | 50-day MA is **above both** the 150-day and 200-day moving averages |
| 5 | Price vs 52-Week Low | Current price is at least **25% above** its 52-week low. (Note: Many best selections will be 100%, 300% or greater above their low before emerging from consolidation) |
| 6 | Price vs 52-Week High | Current price is **within 25%** of its 52-week high (closer to new high = better) |
| 7 | Relative Strength Ranking | RS Ranking is **no less than 70**, but preferably in the 90s |
| 8 | Price vs 50-DMA | Current price is **above the 50-day MA** (exception: "Low Cheat" setups) |

### Additional Trend Template Notes (from workbook):
- RS line should **NOT be in a strong downtrend**
- Prefer RS line in an uptrend for at least **6 weeks, preferably 13 weeks or more**

### Trend Template Variations:
- **1 Month Template:** Use for Power Play setups and early turns
- **1-4 Month Template:** Intermediate confirmation
- **5 Month Template:** Use for stocks in clear, established uptrends

## Part 2: Fundamental Criteria — "3 Key Fundamentals to Watch"

### POSITIVE Indicators (What to Look For):

| Criterion | Threshold/Guideline |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Quarterly EPS Growth | **+20% or more** in most recent 2-3 quarters — the bigger the better |
| Sales Growth | **Increasing** in most recent 2-3 quarters |
| Quarterly Acceleration | Look for quarter-to-quarter sequential acceleration; check 2-quarter average (up 20%) |
| Current vs Historical Growth | Gauge current growth versus **3-5 year growth rate** (look for acceleration above historical rate) |
| Breakout Year | Look for an earnings "breakout year" — current year estimates significantly higher |
| Annual Earnings | Strong annual earnings track record |
| Profit Margins (NPM) | Check if margins are **expanding** — this is the "kicker" that creates explosive EPS growth |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | **15-17%** is a good cutoff for most stocks (compare to industry peers) |
| Code 3 Stocks | Look for stocks with multiple positive fundamental characteristics |

### NEGATIVE Indicators (Red Flags):

| Red Flag | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| Earnings Deceleration | Material quarter-over-quarter slowdown is a warning |
| Eroding Margins | Declining NPM indicates potential trouble ahead |
| Positive EPS / Negative Sales | Growth from cost-cutting has limited lifespan |
| Low Tax Rate | Company showing strong earnings but not paying taxes is suspicious |

## Part 3: Earnings Quality Checks

### Earnings Surprise Analysis:

1. **Initial Response:** Did the stock rally or sell-off on earnings? If it sold off, does it resume its slide after a "dead cat bounce"? Or does it come roaring back?

2. **Subsequent Strength:** How well does the stock hold its gains and resist profit-taking?

3. **Resilience:** Does the stock recover quickly and powerfully after profit-taking? Or did it fail to rally after a pullback?

### Post-Earnings Drift:
- Stocks that beat estimates tend to continue outperforming (60-90 days of drift)
- Watch for earnings gap-ups followed by consolidation forming new buy points

### Earnings Acceleration Pattern:
```
Example from workbook:
Q1: EPS +34%  →  Q2: EPS +12%  →  Q3: EPS +44%  →  Q4: EPS +83%  →  Q5 (Est): +244%

Key: Check most recent 3 quarters + estimates for upcoming 1-2 quarters
```

## Part 4: The Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)™

The VCP is Minervini's signature setup pattern:

### Characteristics:
1. **Multiple Contractions:** Price swings contract in successive waves (e.g., -25% → -15% → -8% → -4%)
2. **Volume Contraction:** Volume typically dries up as the pattern matures
3. **Tight Pivot:** The final contraction creates a low-risk entry point
4. **Time:** Can take 3-6 weeks to form (some emerge after only 10-12 days)

### Pattern Types (Stage 2):
| Pattern | Typical Duration |
|---------|------------------|
| Cup Completion Cheat & Low Cheat | 6-52 weeks |
| Cup with Handle | 7-65 weeks |
| Double Bottom | 7-65 weeks |
| Darvas Box | 4-5 weeks |
| Power Play | 2-6 weeks |

### Power Play Criteria:
1. Explosive price move on huge volume — stock shoots up **100% or more in less than 8 weeks**
2. Can be induced by major news (FDA approval, earnings, new product) or on no news
3. Stock then moves sideways in tight range, **not correcting more than 20%** over 3-6 weeks

## Part 5: What to Look for During Market Corrections

> "More than 96% of superperformance stocks emerge from bear markets or general market corrections."

### Off the General Market Low, Look For:
1. Stocks hitting the **52-week high list**
2. Stocks that **corrected the least** during the market decline (trading within 25% of 52-week high)
3. Stocks that **surged in price** off market lows (largest percentage movers)
4. Stocks that are **base-building/consolidating** within context of a long-term uptrend
5. **Proliferation of stocks** setting up and emerging through proper buy points
6. **Accumulation** in major averages when leaders start breaking out

## Part 7: Risk Management Rules (From Workbook)

### During Difficult Periods:
1. Get **off margin immediately** if using leverage
2. **Reduce exposure** — lower overall capital commitment
3. **Tighten stop-losses** — if normally 7-8%, cut at 4-5%
4. **Take smaller profits** — if normally 15-20%, take at 10-12%

### Key Principle:
> "DO NOT WIDEN YOUR STOPS!"

### Reward Must Outweigh Risk (3-Year Compounding Table):

| Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | 3-Yr Total Return | Annual |
|--------|--------|--------|-------------------|--------|
| +30% | +30% | -30% | 18.3% | 5.8% |
| +50% | +50% | -50% | 12.5% | 4.0% |
| +70% | +70% | -70% | -13.3% | -4.7% |

**Lesson:** One large loss can wipe out years of gains — risk control is paramount.

## Summary: The SEPA Screening Priorities

**Non-Negotiables (must have):**
1. Stage 2 uptrend confirmed (Trend Template passed)
2. Positive quarterly earnings growth (+20%+)
3. Sales growth present
4. Margins expanding or stable
5. RS line trending up

**Nice-to-Haves (improves probability):**
1. Earnings acceleration (current > historical growth rate)
2. Earnings surprise with positive market reaction
3. Small float (<30M shares)
4. Recent catalyst
5. Near 52-week high

**Avoid:**
1. Stage 3 or 4 stocks
2. Decelerating earnings
3. Negative earnings surprises with poor recovery
4. Stocks lagging the market (low RS)
5. Stocks below 200-DMA
